# Business Problem

## The Challenge Facing Fraud Operations Teams

Modern fraud teams are not failing because they lack fraud models. They are failing because they cannot act on the signals those models produce.

A typical mid-size bank processes hundreds of thousands of transactions per day. A fraud model flags 2–5% as suspicious. That generates thousands of alerts. The investigations team has capacity for a fraction of those. Without a system to prioritize, assign, and track each alert through to resolution, the fraud team is overwhelmed — and fraudsters exploit the gap.

---

## The Four Operational Failures

### 1. Alert Overload Without Prioritization
Fraud models output a score. They do not tell an investigator which alert to open first. Teams with no risk-tiering system treat a score-55 alert the same as a score-98 alert. High-risk cases wait. Losses grow.

### 2. No Case Ownership or SLA Visibility
When an alert is not formally assigned to an investigator with a deadline, it falls through the cracks. There is no accountability. There is no SLA. There is no way to measure whether the team is functioning.

### 3. Reactive Detection — No Early Warning
Traditional fraud platforms only confirm fraud after it happens. By then, the loss is booked. A proactive watchlist that monitors accounts showing early behavioral risk signals — rising alert frequency, deteriorating fraud scores, unusual merchant activity — allows teams to intervene before the fraud event occurs.

### 4. Invisible Loss Exposure
Fraud leaders cannot answer basic executive questions: How much fraud did we catch? How much did we miss? What is our potential loss exposure? Without structured reporting, fraud operations cannot justify headcount, tooling, or process changes.

---

## The Business Impact of These Failures

| Failure | Business Consequence |
|---|---|
| No prioritization | High-risk cases delayed, losses confirmed |
| No case management | SLA breaches, regulatory risk, no audit trail |
| No early warning | Fraud losses are unavoidable, not preventable |
| No loss reporting | Leadership cannot measure or fund the fraud team |

---

## What This Platform Solves

This platform builds the operational infrastructure around fraud detection:

**Detect** suspicious transactions using a scored fraud model.

**Assess** risk tier so investigators know what to open first.

**Assign** cases to investigators with SLA deadlines.

**Investigate** cases through a defined lifecycle: Open → Investigating → Escalated → Resolved.

**Watch** accounts showing rising risk signals before fraud is confirmed.

**Escalate** high-risk cases with a documented audit trail.

**Report** fraud losses, exposure, SLA compliance, and trends to leadership.

**Review** fraud patterns monthly in a structured management report.

## The Business Value

A fraud team using this platform can:

- Reduce time-to-investigate high-risk cases by prioritizing Critical and High tier alerts
- Prevent fraud losses by acting on watchlist signals before confirmed fraud
- Maintain regulatory compliance through audit trails and SLA tracking
- Quantify fraud operations performance with structured KPIs

This is not a fraud model. This is a fraud operations platform built around a fraud model.
